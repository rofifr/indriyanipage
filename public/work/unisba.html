Work of Group 4

Unlocking the Impact of Organizational Experience on Workforce

Productivity: A Study on 2018 Graduates of FMIPA UNISBA

The actual project was conducted in Indonesian language, as the research focused on the FMIPA UNISBA 2018 graduates and their experiences in the local context. We conducted the survey, collected data, and performed the analysis using Indonesian language materials. However, for the purpose of sharing our project and findings with a wider audience, we have translated and presented the information in English to ensure accessibility and understanding for readers who are interested in the topic of organizational experience and workforce productivity.

The background of our research relates to the importance of organizational experience in enhancing workforce productivity. In this context, our focus is on the 2018 cohort of FMIPA UNISBA graduates. We are interested in understanding how the organizational experience of these graduates can impact their productivity in the workplace.

Initially, we conducted a survey involving the 2018 FMIPA UNISBA graduates to gather data on their organizational experience during their studies. We also collected information about their current level of productivity in the workplace. With this data, we had the opportunity to examine whether there is a relationship between organizational experience and productivity.

The data analysis process began with preparing and cleaning the obtained data. We removed irrelevant or duplicate data and selected the most relevant data for our analysis. Then, we organized the data into tables and graphs to facilitate understanding and analysis.

Next, we performed descriptive analysis using statistical methods to gain initial insights into the variables we were studying. We calculated the means, medians, and modes of the organizational experience and workforce productivity variables, allowing us to observe general trends and patterns that may emerge from the data.

Subsequently, we applied simple regression analysis to test the relationship between organizational experience (independent variable) and workforce productivity (dependent variable). We calculated regression coefficients and constants and conducted hypothesis tests to determine if organizational experience has a significant influence on workforce productivity.

After performing the statistical analysis, we evaluated the research findings and concluded whether organizational experience has a positive impact on workforce productivity. We also provided recommendations for students preparing to enter the job market based on our findings.

As a group of researchers, we recognize the importance of disseminating our findings to the public. Therefore, we decided to create an informative and engaging blog article. This article will cover the background of our research, the analysis process we conducted, as well as the results and recommendations derived. We hope our blog article can provide insights and inspiration for readers interested in the topic of organizational experience and workforce productivity, especially for students preparing to enter the job market.

Through this journey, we have learned a great deal about the significance of organizational experience and how it can impact workforce productivity. We have also developed our analytical skills in data analysis and drawing conclusions that can be applied in the context of the working world.
